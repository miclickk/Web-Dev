Kangaroos

Jump to sections: Locomotion, Diet

The kangaroo is a marsupial from the family Macropodidae (macropods, meaning "large foot"). In common use, the term is used to describe the largest species from this family, especially those of the genus Macropus. Kangaroos are endemic to Australia.

Locomotion

Kangaroos move by hopping. This is very energy efficient at high speed, and they can travel long distances using powerful hind legs.

Diet

Most kangaroos are herbivores. They mainly eat grasses, leaves, and shrubs, and they can survive in dry conditions with limited water.
